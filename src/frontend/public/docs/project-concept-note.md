# 📝 Concept Note

---

## 📋 Project Overview

| **Section** | **Details** |
|---|---|
| **Project Title** | Establishment and Operationalization of UBUNIFU SACCO to Support Technology Startups and Creative Enterprises in Uganda |
| **Implementing Organization** | UBUNIFU SACCO Ltd. |
| **Contact Person** | Mucunguzi Moses, Programme Manager |
| **Project Location** | National: Across Uganda, with regional chapters / branches in major innovation hubs (e.g. Kampala, Mbarara, Gulu, Mbale) |
| **Duration** | 5 years (January 2026 – December 2031) |
| **Total Budget** | UGX 50,000,000,000 |

> 💡 **Insight:** The UBUNIFU SACCO will serve as a specialized cooperative financial and support institution for technology startups, digital innovators, and creative enterprises. It seeks to fill the gap left by traditional SACCOs and commercial banks, which often do not cater to high-risk, early-stage creative or tech ventures.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

1. Establish an operational SACCO infrastructure (governance, systems, branch/agent networks, digital platform) for tech and creative enterprises.
2. Mobilize capital through member contributions, share issuance, private investors, and grants.
3. Provide access to affordable credit, working capital, and innovation grants to members.
4. Facilitate networking, mentorship, incubation support, and market linkages.
5. Strengthen member capacity in financial literacy, business planning, and commercialization of innovations.

---

## ⚙️ Key Activities

1. Develop SACCO governance, bylaws, board structures, internal controls, and strategic frameworks.
2. Build and deploy a SACCO management information system and digital channels (mobile, web) for member access.
3. Capital raising: membership drives, share issuance, partner engagements, grant proposals.
4. Design tailored financial products (microloans, seed capital, bridging finance, savings schemes) suited for creative/tech ventures.
5. Establish regional branches or agent networks in key innovation hubs.
6. Provide mentorship, business incubation, peer networking, pitch events, investor connections.
7. Train members in financial literacy, business model development, commercialization strategies.
8. Monitor, evaluate, and iterate services based on member feedback, performance metrics, and market conditions.

---

## 🏆 Expected Outcomes / Results

1. At least 50,000 members (tech and creative enterprises) enrolled.
2. Disbursement of loans, grants, or support to a target number of startups/creative projects.
3. Improved business metrics for members (revenue growth, employment, sustainability).
4. Enhanced connectivity across the innovation ecosystem; mentor, investor, incubator linkages.
5. A financially viable SACCO by year 3, able to sustain operations from member revenues and returns.

---

## 🤝 Key Stakeholders

1. Members (tech startups, creative enterprises, youth-led ventures)
2. Board of UBUNIFU SACCO
3. Government agencies (Ministry of ICT, Ministry of Finance)
4. Innovation hubs, accelerators, incubators
5. Private investors, venture capital firms, angel networks
6. Development partners and donors (local & international)
7. Financial regulators, cooperative associations

---

## 📊 Monitoring & Evaluation

1. Quarterly and annual reports on membership growth, loan uptake, repayment rates, defaults, member satisfaction.
2. Site visits and audits of branch/agent operations.
3. Feedback mechanisms (member surveys, focus group discussions).
4. Performance benchmarks against peer SACCOs and industry KPIs.
5. Midterm and endline evaluations to assess impact on member businesses and innovation outcomes.

---

## ♻️ Sustainability Plan

1. Adopt a shareholding model so that members own equity in the SACCO, fostering commitment.
2. Generate revenue from interest on loans, service fees (kept affordable), and advisory services.
3. Establish prudent risk management, loan recovery policies, and reserves.
4. Create partnerships with investors, donors, and government for co-funding and guarantee schemes.
5. Continuously adapt services to member needs to retain relevance and competitive advantage.
6. Strengthen governance, board accountability, and oversight to maintain trust and financial stability.
