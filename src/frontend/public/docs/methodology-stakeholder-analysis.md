# Stakeholder Analysis

## Government and Regulatory Authorities

| **Stakeholder** | **Role** | **Interest** | **Influence** |
|-----------------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Ministry of Trade, Industry and Cooperatives | Registration, oversight | Strong cooperative sector | High |
| Uganda Microfinance Regulatory Authority (UMRA) | Licensing, compliance | Financial stability | High |
| Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) | Legal registration | Formal business registration | Medium |
| Ministry of ICT and National Guidance | Policy, funding | Digital transformation | High |
| Ministry of Finance | Funding, policy | Economic development | High |

## Primary Beneficiaries (Members)

| **Stakeholder** | **Description** | **Interests** | **Influence** |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|---------------|
| Tech Startups | Early-stage tech companies | Access to capital, mentorship, networks | High |
| Digital Entrepreneurs | Online business owners | Working capital, digital tools | High |
| Innovators/Inventors | Product developers | Prototype funding, IP support | High |
| Creative Professionals | Artists, designers, filmmakers | Equipment, monetization | High |
| Youth-led Enterprises | Businesses owned by under-35s | Startup support, peer learning | High |
| Women in Tech | Female entrepreneurs | Gender-inclusive services | Medium-High |

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

- **Feedback loops** through surveys and suggestion boxes
- **Stakeholder forums** for dialogue and co-creation
- **Co-design sessions** for product development
- **Annual general meetings** for member governance
- **Partnership committees** with key collaborators
